Pasta Puttanesca Recipe

Description

The name of this dish, Puttanesca, actually derives from an old Italian swear word...

But despite its seemingly crass origins, it's a delicious and easy to make dish featuring tomatoes, black olives and anchovies.

This is a simplified version, however capers and onions can also be included. It is best enjoyed with a seasoning of parmesan cheese.

Ingredients

  • 400g tin of chopped tomatoes
  • 75g of black pitted olives
  • A tin of anchovies
  • A clove of garlic
  • A quarter teaspoon of basil
  • A quarter teaspoon of chilli powder
  • 100g of spaghetti for each person served

Steps

  1. Before you start, boil the water in the kettle so it is ready
  2. Add the tin of chopped tomatoes to a pot, heated on the stove hob
  3. Remove the water from the black olives with a sive and add to pot
  4. Add the entire tin of anchovies to mixture and stir until they have melted
  5. Crush the garlic in a garlic press and add it
  6. Add a quarter teaspoon of basil to the mixture
  7. Then add a quarter chilli powder
  8. If you would like, at this stage other spices can be added
  9. Measure out the spaghetti with scales and add it to another separate pot
  10. Add boiling water to this new pot with the spaghetti and leave for 8 to 9 minutes
  11. Once the time is up, remove the water from the spaghetti with a sive and mix the spaghetti with the sauce
  12. Serve while hot and enjoy!
